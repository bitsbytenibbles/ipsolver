The Continuous Review Problems module analyzes the (s, Q) inventory ordering policy under which the inventory position (on-hand + on-order â€“ backorder) is monitored continuously and an order of size Q is placed whenever the inventory position hits the reorder point, s. The model considered in this module is the approximate treatment of the continuous review with mixture of backorder and lost sales based on the approximation of Hadley and Whitin (Analysis of inventory system, Prentice Hall, 1963). The problem is to find the optimal s and Q that minimize the approximate expected total cost per unit of time. The modules used 12 probability distributions to describe the lead time demand. They are:

  1. Continuous Uniform
  2. Discrete Uniform
  3. Erlang
  4. Exponential
  5. Gamma
  6. Laplace
  7. Lognormal
  8. Negative Binomial
  9. Normal
  10. Poisson
  11. Triangular
  12. Weibull.

Furthermore, the user can also enter his/her own empirical discrete probability distribution by entering the set of possible lead time demand values and their associated probabilities. The module also provides a solution to the problem under the free distribution approach which only assumes the knowledge of the first two moments of demand distribution and not necessarily its form.
