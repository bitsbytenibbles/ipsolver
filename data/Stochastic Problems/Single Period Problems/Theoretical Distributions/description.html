The Single Period Problems module addresses the classical inventory model with uncertain demand and single opportunity for placing an order often referred to in the literature as “the single period”, ‘newsvendor’, ‘newsboy’, or ‘Christmas Tree’ problem. Such a problem arises when, for example, a store manager has only one opportunity to place an order for a product to be sold during a short selling period (smaller than the procurement lead time) without knowing for certain the demand for the product. Inventory cannot be added later in case the demand for the period exceeds the amount ordered. A holding cost is charged for every unit not sold and held at the end of the period. On the other hand, when demand exceeds the ordering quantity, the store incurs a shortage penalty cost. The problem is to find the optimal target inventory to set at the beginning of the period that maximizes the expected profit under both situations with zero an non-zero ordering cost. The modules used 12 probability distributions to describe the total demand over the selling period. They are:

  1. Continuous Uniform
  2. Discrete Uniform
  3. Erlang
  4. Exponential
  5. Gamma
  6. Laplace
  7. Lognormal
  8. Negative Binomial
  9. Normal
  10. Poisson
  11. Triangular
  12. Weibull.

Furthermore, the user can also enter his/her own empirical discrete probability distribution by entering the set of possible demand values and their associated probabilities. The module also provides a solution to the problem under the free distribution approach which only assumes the knowledge of the first two moments of demand distribution and not necessarily its form.
